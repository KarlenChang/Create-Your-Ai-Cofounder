# Risk Iteration Path Reference

Load this when the user already has a Lean Canvas and wants to pressure-test, stress-test, or analyze it.

Source: Ash Maurya's Running Lean. See also: https://www.playinglean.com/blogs/playing-lean-blog/what-is-the-risk-iteration-path

## The Three Risk Chains

Each chain is a 4-step sequence. You iterate through one chain at a time. Start with Product Risk (most founders start here by default), but let the user choose.

---

### Product Risk — "Getting the right product"

Sequence: **Problem → Solution → UVP → Key Metrics**

| Step | Box | Pressure-Test Question |
|------|-----|----------------------|
| 1 | **Problem** | "Do you have a problem worth solving? Would customers describe it this way? Have you talked to them?" |
| 2 | **Solution** | "Does your MVP actually solve the problem? What's the smallest thing you could build to test this?" |
| 3 | **UVP** | "Can you convert a warm prospect in a 20-minute face-to-face conversation? If not, your UVP isn't sharp enough." |
| 4 | **Key Metrics** | "Are the numbers moving? Which metric tells you the product is working — and which is the one that's lagging?" |

**Testing method:** Problem Interview → Solution Interview (demo) → MVP at small scale → Roll out and monitor metrics

---

### Customer Risk — "Building a path to customers"

Sequence: **Customer Segments → Early Adopters → Channels (outbound) → Channels (inbound)**

| Step | Box | Pressure-Test Question |
|------|-----|----------------------|
| 1 | **Customer Segments** | "Who exactly has this problem? How many of them are there? Are they a real market or a hypothetical one?" |
| 2 | **Early Adopters** | "Who has the most acute pain right now? Who would pay today, not someday? Who's your earlyvangelist?" |
| 3 | **Channels (outbound)** | "How do you reach them today — cold calls, ads, events? What's your customer acquisition cost?" |
| 4 | **Channels (inbound)** | "Can you build a scalable pull channel — SEO, content, referrals? How do you lower CAC over time?" |

**Testing method:** Customer interviews → Identify early adopters → Test outbound channels → Experiment with inbound channels

---

### Market Risk — "Building a viable business"

Sequence: **Existing Alternatives → Revenue (verbal) → Revenue (actual) → Cost Structure**

| Step | Box | Pressure-Test Question |
|------|-----|----------------------|
| 1 | **Existing Alternatives** | "What do customers use today? How much do they pay for it? What's their willingness to pay?" |
| 2 | **Revenue (verbal)** | "Before you build anything, ask: would you pay $X for this? Get verbal commitments or letters of intent." |
| 3 | **Revenue (actual)** | "Now that you have an MVP, do people actually pay? Test pricing — go up, go down, try different models." |
| 4 | **Cost Structure** | "As your model becomes clear, iterate on the costs that make it profitable. What's the unit economics?" |

**Testing method:** Competitor analysis → Verbal commitment tests → Pricing experiments → Cost optimization

---

## How to Run a Pressure Test

1. **Ask the user which chain to start with** (or default to Product Risk)
2. **Walk through the chain step by step** — one box at a time
3. **At each box, ask the pressure-test question** from the table
4. **Listen for weak spots** — where the user hesitates, contradicts themselves, or says "I don't know"
5. **Flag the weakest link** — the chain is only as strong as its weakest step
6. **Suggest the next experiment** — what specific test would validate or invalidate this step?
7. **Move to the next chain** after completing one (or stop if the user wants to)

## Key Insight

The chains are sequential — you don't jump around. If your Problem is weak, your Solution will be too. If your Customer Segments are wrong, your Channels won't work. Find the crack in the chain first.
